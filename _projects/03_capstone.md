## Background
This was my senior year capstone project. My teammates and I were assigned to a project thats goal was to help move U of M in the direction of fielding a team for the ROBOCUP Humanoid League. A research challenge that competes every year to field a team of Humanoid soccer playing robots with the eventual goal of reaching parity with FIFA world cup champions. This is of course a very lofty goal but soccer is a useful testing ground for humanoid robots.

## Our Focus
Due to the constraints of a single semester class we decide to focus on implementing basic team strategy logic in simulation. We used the official (but in beta at the time of writing this) Mujoco Simulation made by the ROBOCUP foundation. A lot of time was spent getting the basics such as walking and path planning working using open source examples. Our scenario was two robots attempting to score while avoiding three robots on the other team treated as static obstacles.
